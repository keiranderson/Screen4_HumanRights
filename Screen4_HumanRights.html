Disseminates

Analysts differ on whether globalization harms or helps human rights. Globalization helps disseminate human rights ideas around the world. Global communication, cultural mixing, migration, and education inform people about rights. Globalization spurs economic development, which often leads people to expect respect for their personal freedom and right to participate in the nationâ€™s politics. Challenges

Yet, globalization also creates challenges. The race to the bottom means that worker rights and the social security rights of the people can be neglected. Corporate demands for lower wages and lighter regulations reduce living standards and make the workplace more hazardous. Inequality has widened over the last few decades, and a billion people continue to live at bare subsistence levels. Austerity programs imposed by international financial institutions entail reducing public programs for education and health. The global war against terrorist organizations displays numerous violations of human rights, including the use of torture and arbitrary detention against suspected terrorists.
